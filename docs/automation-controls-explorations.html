Automation controls — outside-the-box explorations

Same automation engine, but driven by controls built for hands-on live use instead of sliders and dropdowns. Each is interactive; the oscilloscope and resulting formula update live. Everything is still time-based and slow by default; the step sequencer is the one that rides the tapped tempo.

Flick wheel (jog)

DJ-platter feel: grab and spin. Flick speed sets the rate, direction sets which way it cycles — then let go and it coasts down on friction. Throw it for a fast sweep that naturally settles, like a turntable.

More ideas worth a prototype (not built here)

Performance pads

Big momentary/latch buttons: hold to engage a motion (release = snap back), or tap to latch. Perfect for build-ups and drops, like Ableton/Serato pads.

MIDI learn

Right-click a param → wiggle a physical knob/fader → it's bound. The on-screen control becomes a mirror of the hardware. The end-goal for live.

Tilt / gesture

On a tablet, device tilt drives depth; shake triggers a random reseed of the noise shapes (Drift/Wander).

Audio-reactive envelope

Mic/line level (or a band split) drives the param — kick pumps depth, hats flick the RGB split. The VJ classic.

Springs & pendulums

Beyond the bouncing ball: drag-release a spring for a damped overshoot, or chain pendulums for coupled, never-repeating motion.

How these relate to the formula engine

XY pad, knobs and the macro dial just move the same sine/shape parameters, so they compile to the exact same closed-form formula as the sliders (the macro dial simply remaps one input across several params). The flick wheel is closed-form too — it only drives the LFO rate — but its phase is live-integrated, so its export is a steady sine at whatever Hz it has coasted to. Draw-your-own, the step sequencer, the touch ribbon and the bouncing ball aren't closed forms — they're a stored table or a live/physical signal (recorded curve, 16 levels, eased finger position, a gravity sim) replayed against t or beat. In the real app those would either bake to a small lookup the engine samples, or stay preview-only — flagged so live↔export parity stays honest.
